ARC Raiders Ferro Weapon Guide — Stats, Best Build & Tips

March 6, 2026  ·  8 min read  ·  Weapon guide & loadout analysis

The Ferro is the most widely used weapon in ARC Raiders — and for good reason. It's the go-to starting weapon for new players and remains a competitive option even at high levels thanks to its forgiving recoil, solid damage per shot, and universal ammo availability. This guide covers everything you need to know about running the Ferro effectively.

Ferro Weapon Overview

The Ferro is a mid-range assault rifle classified as the standard "bread and butter" weapon of ARC Raiders. It uses common ammo, has predictable recoil, and performs reliably across all engagement distances from 20–80m. It is the weapon most new players will start with and the one you'll find in the largest number of community loadouts.

Why the Ferro is the Best Starter Weapon

The Ferro's biggest advantage is accessibility. Ammo drops frequently across all map zones, crafting costs are minimal, and the weapon itself is affordable for early-game loadouts. Unlike specialist weapons that demand precise playstyles, the Ferro works for both ARC kills and player engagements.

  • Low recoil: Easy to control even without aim-assist attachments
  • Common ammo: Never struggle for resupply mid-raid
  • Versatile range: Handles both close and medium engagements
  • Budget-friendly: Best return on investment for early-game survival
  • Universal pairing: Works with any skill tree build (Mobility, Survival, Conditioning)

Best Skill Tree Build for Ferro Players

Because the Ferro is a versatile all-rounder, it pairs best with a balanced or survival-focused skill tree. Players running the Ferro for loot runs benefit most from Survival investment; players using it for PvP should lean Mobility.

Recommended: Ferro Balanced Build

32 Mobility / 28 Survival / 15 Conditioning — The All-Rounder allocation works perfectly with the Ferro's versatile nature.

  • Marathon Runner (Mobility) — Sprint between engagements faster
  • Field Medic (Survival) — Heal faster after taking hits
  • Scrapping Speed I–III (Survival) — Loot faster while carrying the Ferro
  • Effortless Roll (Mobility) — Dodge more freely in close-quarters
  • Carry Weight I (Conditioning) — Support additional ammo and gear
Loot-run variant: If you're using the Ferro purely for extraction runs (low-risk looting), shift to 40 Survival / 20 Mobility / 15 Conditioning. Prioritize Scrapping Speed and Backpack Capacity to maximize what you extract per raid.

Ferro Combat Tactics

Against ARCs (PvE)

The Ferro excels against standard ARC units at medium range. Keep distance between 30–60m, aim for head and chest zones, and use terrain for cover between bursts. The manageable recoil makes it easy to stay on target during multi-hit sequences.

Against Players (PvP)

In player-vs-player scenarios, the Ferro is serviceable but not dominant. Its strength is consistency — you won't miss shots due to uncontrollable recoil. Use it defensively: take engagements only when you have positional advantage or the opponent is reloading.

Limitation: The Ferro struggles at long range against players using dedicated sniper weapons like the Venator or Osprey. If you're pushing into sniper sight lines, close distance quickly using Mobility nodes before engaging.

Extraction Priority

When extracting, the Ferro's light weight and common ammo mean you have budget left for high-value loot. Prioritize gear with high sell value over extra Ferro ammo — you can always resupply next raid.

Ferro Loadout Recommendations

Budget Loadout (Early Game)

  • Primary: Ferro (base or Mk1)
  • Secondary: Rattler pistol for emergency close-range
  • Focus: Survival skill tree — maximize what you extract

Mid-Game Loadout

  • Primary: Ferro Mk2 with attachments
  • Secondary: Stitcher for CQC backup
  • Focus: Balanced build — rotate between looting and PvP capability

Ferro vs Other Weapons

Ferro vs Stitcher: The Stitcher wins in close-range burst engagements. The Ferro is better at medium range and more ammo-efficient. If you're playing solo loot runs, the Ferro is safer; if you're expecting close engagements, bring the Stitcher.

Ferro vs Anvil: The Anvil hits harder per shot but has slower fire rate. The Ferro is more forgiving if you miss — the Anvil punishes missed shots more severely. New players should start with the Ferro before graduating to the Anvil.

Ferro vs Venator: These occupy different niches. The Venator is a long-range sniper; the Ferro handles mid-range. Most mid-to-advanced players carry both: Ferro for general combat, Venator for securing long-range targets.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Ferro still good in late game?

Yes. While end-game players often upgrade to higher-tier weapons for dedicated roles, the Ferro remains viable as a budget-friendly all-rounder. Its key strength at all stages is reliability: you can always afford it, always find ammo, and always use it.

What attachments should I use on the Ferro?

Prioritize attachments that reduce recoil and extend effective range first. A vertical grip (if available) and mid-range optic significantly improve the Ferro's performance. Avoid heavy suppressors on the Ferro — they reduce velocity and hurt at-range performance.

What skill tree branch pairs best with the Ferro?

For solo players, Survival. For PvP-focused players, Mobility. For team play, a balanced Conditioning/Survival mix lets you sustain longer and loot more between engagements. See the best overall build guide for full allocation details.
