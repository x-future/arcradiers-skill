ARC Raiders Stitcher Guide — Is It the Best CQC Weapon?

March 6, 2026  ·  8 min read  ·  Close-quarters combat analysis

The Stitcher is ARC Raiders' signature close-quarters combat weapon — a high fire-rate SMG that shreds enemies inside buildings and tight corridors. Community data consistently shows it as one of the most-used weapons across all skill levels, particularly in loadouts designed for indoor zones. Is it the best CQC weapon in the game? This guide breaks down the full picture.

Stitcher Weapon Overview

The Stitcher is a compact SMG with an extremely high fire rate and moderate-to-good damage per bullet. Its effective range is limited to close-quarters (roughly 5–30m), but within that range it dominates most engagements through sheer DPS. It's one of the cheapest weapons to maintain thanks to common ammo availability.

Why the Stitcher Dominates Indoor Combat

Indoor zones in ARC Raiders have tight corners, short sight lines, and dense loot areas that funnel players into close-range encounters. The Stitcher was built for exactly this. Its high fire rate allows you to maximize damage before opponents can react, and its compact size makes navigating indoor environments faster.

  • Highest DPS at close range: No other weapon deals damage this fast below 20m
  • Fast ADS (aim-down-sights): Quick target acquisition in tight spaces
  • Low recoil in short bursts: Easy to keep shots on target at CQC distance
  • Budget-friendly: Ammo is everywhere; the gun itself is affordable
  • Lightweight: Leaves inventory budget for more loot

Best Skill Tree Build for Stitcher Players

The Stitcher is the perfect weapon for an aggressive Mobility-focused build. You want to close distance fast, dodge incoming fire, and disengage before opponents can adjust. A pure Mobility build amplifies everything the Stitcher does well.

  • Marathon Runner (Mobility) — Sprint to close distance before enemies can react
  • Effortless Roll (Mobility) — Dodge return fire during the burst exchange
  • Light Footed (Mobility) — Reduce footstep audio; approach without telegraphing

Aggression tip: The Stitcher rewards initiating engagements. If you let enemies set up, their longer-range weapons negate your DPS advantage. Always push first — use Mobility nodes to close distance before the opponent can create space.

Stitcher Combat Tactics

The Gap Close

The Stitcher's greatest skill is initiating from an unexpected angle. Use sprint + vault to enter rooms from windows or elevated entries rather than doors (which enemies watch). Marathon Runner and Nimble Climber make this approach faster than opponents expect.

Burst Fire at Medium Range

While the Stitcher is primarily a CQC weapon, you can extend its range slightly with controlled 3–5 round bursts at 25–40m. The high fire rate causes spread at full-auto medium range — short bursts keep shots grouped on target.

Reload Awareness

The Stitcher's magazine empties fast. After every engagement, assess whether you have time to reload before the next contact. Running into a second enemy on an empty magazine is the most common Stitcher death. Use obstacles for cover during reloads.

Is the Stitcher the Best CQC Weapon?

Short answer: Yes, for most players in most CQC situations.

The Stitcher's combination of high DPS, budget cost, and ammo availability makes it the default best option for close-quarters combat. The only weapon that rivals it in CQC is the Anvil at specific engagement distances (10–20m) where its higher damage-per-bullet compensates for its slower fire rate. But the Stitcher is more forgiving and easier to use effectively.

For pure close-range dominance, the Stitcher is the answer. For mixed indoor/outdoor loadouts, pair the Stitcher with a Ferro or Venator secondary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I use the Stitcher or the Ferro for solo runs?

Depends on the map. Indoor-heavy routes: Stitcher. Mixed indoor/outdoor routes: Ferro. The Ferro's medium-range capability makes it safer when you might unexpectedly hit open terrain. Many experienced players bring both — Stitcher primary for interiors, Ferro as backup for outdoor escapes.

What attachments work best on the Stitcher?

Extended magazine and vertical grip are the highest-priority attachments. Extended mag reduces the frequency of dangerous mid-fight reloads. A red-dot or holographic sight over iron sights improves target acquisition in low-light indoor environments.

How does the Stitcher work with a Survival skill tree?

Survivability-focused players running a balanced build can absolutely use the Stitcher — just accept that you'll be more reactive than aggressive. Post-engagement healing efficiency from Survival nodes compensates for the higher damage intake from aggressive close-range play.
