ARC Raiders Kettle Weapon Guide — Stats, Loadout & Best Build

March 6, 2026  ·  8 min read  ·  Mid-range weapon analysis

The Kettle is one of ARC Raiders' more versatile mid-range weapons, appearing frequently in community loadouts for players who want a weapon that handles both looting runs and unexpected combat encounters without over-specializing in either direction. This guide covers the Kettle's strengths, limitations, and the best skill tree setups to run with it.

Kettle Weapon Overview

The Kettle is a mid-range, moderate fire-rate rifle positioned as a step up from pure budget weapons without the premium price of end-game options. Community loadout data shows it popular among players running solo exploration routes where encountering both ARC enemies and other players is possible and avoiding both equally.

Who Should Use the Kettle

The Kettle is ideal for solo players who prioritize survival and extraction over maximum combat performance. It doesn't excel in pure PvP against dedicated combat weapons like the Tempest or Anvil, but it performs adequately in defensive encounters while leaving inventory budget for high-value loot.

  • Solo looters: Reliable enough for ARC kills, budget-friendly enough to protect extraction economy
  • New players graduating from the Ferro: Similar handling, slightly better combat performance
  • Low-risk run specialists: Players who want a weapon in case of contact without full combat investment

Best Skill Tree Build for Kettle Players

The Kettle pairs naturally with a Survival-dominant build. Its users are typically prioritizing loot extraction over combat, so Survival nodes that improve looting speed, inventory capacity, and stealth noise reduction complement the Kettle's role.

Kettle defensive philosophy: Don't seek combat — let the Kettle be your last resort. If you encounter a player, your goal is to disengage first. The Kettle's versatility means if you can't escape, you have a functional weapon. But your loadout value (full loot bags) makes engagement too costly.

Kettle Combat Tactics

Defensive Engagement

When you must fight with the Kettle, use terrain. Find cover, wait for opponent movement errors, and place deliberate shots rather than trading openly. The Kettle's moderate damage per shot requires landing most hits — sloppy aim at exposed ranges works against you.

ARC Enemy Efficiency

Against ARC enemies, the Kettle is efficient. Standard ARC units don't require premium weapon performance — they reward consistent shot placement which the Kettle provides. Target weak points for maximum efficiency and keep ammo usage per ARC kill low to maintain your budget.

When to Disengage

If you encounter a heavily armed player running premium weapons, disengage immediately. Your Kettle + full loot bags is a losing proposition against someone running a Tempest or Venator in a direct fight. Use Mobility nodes and terrain to create distance and extract via alternative routes.

Kettle vs Ferro — Are They the Same?

The Kettle and Ferro occupy similar roles but the Kettle offers slightly better performance at a modest cost increase. The key differences:

  • Damage: Kettle hits slightly harder per shot
  • Handling: Comparable — both are beginner-accessible
  • Cost: Kettle costs more to run; Ferro is the true budget option
  • Community rating: Both are well-regarded for their respective tiers

If you're already comfortable with the Ferro and want a minor upgrade without committing to the Anvil's semi-auto playstyle, the Kettle is a natural lateral step.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Kettle worth using over the Ferro?

If you can comfortably afford the slight cost increase, yes. The marginal performance improvement is worth it for players in mid-game progression. If you're still building your economy, stay on the Ferro until you have the budget cushion to absorb Kettle losses comfortably.

What's the best secondary for the Kettle?

The Rattler is the default choice for Kettle users running loot builds — it covers CQC emergencies without budget impact. See the Rattler guide for why it's the natural Kettle companion.

Does the Kettle work in PvP?

Adequately, but not optimally. Against unprepared opponents or in defensive encounters, yes. Against dedicated PvP loadouts with premium weapons, the Kettle will underperform. For pure PvP, see the PvP build guide for better weapon recommendations.
